A Legacy of Innovation

Key Milestones

1968
Intel Founded
Gordon Moore & Robert Noyce establish Intel Corporation in Santa Clara, CA.
1968

Intel Founded

Gordon Moore and Robert Noyce co-found Intel Corporation, laying the groundwork for the global semiconductor revolution. The company's mission: deliver innovative technology to connect and enrich lives worldwide.

Origins
1971
First Microprocessor
Intel releases the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor.
1971

Intel 4004

Intel introduces the world's first commercial microprocessor — the Intel 4004. Containing 2,300 transistors, this 4-bit chip ignited a computing revolution and transformed the semiconductor industry forever.

Innovation
1978
8086 Processor
The Intel 8086 launches the x86 architecture still powering PCs today.
1978

8086 Processor

The Intel 8086 processor introduces the x86 architecture — a 16-bit powerhouse that defined personal computing for decades and forms the foundation of the modern PC ecosystem, still in widespread use today.

Architecture
1985
386 Processor
Intel 386 brings 32-bit multitasking to personal computers.
1985

Intel 386

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, enabling true multitasking for personal computers. With 275,000 transistors, it ushered in a new era of performance and consumer computing capability.

Performance
1994
Pentium® Era
The Pentium processor becomes synonymous with PC performance worldwide.
1994

Pentium® Era

Intel's Pentium processor brand transforms consumer marketing of computing. Featuring 3.1 million transistors and a 66 MHz clock, it becomes one of the most recognizable tech brands globally, driving mass adoption of PCs.

Consumer
2006
Core™ Duo Launch
Intel Core™ architecture delivers dual-core power efficiency for laptops.
2006

Intel Core™ Duo

Intel's Core™ microarchitecture redefines the performance-per-watt frontier. Dual-core processors dramatically improve laptop battery life while doubling computational throughput — a key sustainability breakthrough in chip design.

Efficiency
2012
Green Goals Set
Intel commits to achieving net positive water use by 2030.
2012

Sustainability Pledge

Intel sets ambitious environmental targets including net positive water use, zero waste to landfill, and 100% renewable electricity. This marks a turning point in the tech industry's approach to corporate environmental responsibility.

Sustainability
2016
100% Renewable
Intel achieves 100% renewable electricity across U.S. operations.
2016

Renewable Milestone

Intel reaches a landmark sustainability achievement: 100% renewable electricity across all U.S. facilities. The company becomes one of the largest corporate purchasers of green energy in the United States.

Renewable Energy
2020
RISE Strategy
Intel launches RISE: Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling tech.
2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy — Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling — with bold 2030 goals addressing climate action, water stewardship, zero waste, and supply chain responsibility across its global footprint.

ESG Strategy
2030
Net Zero Goal
Intel targets net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across global operations.
2030 Goal

Net Zero Emissions

Intel commits to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across all Scope 1 and Scope 2 operations by 2040, with aggressive interim milestones by 2030 — including 4 billion gallons of water restoration and zero waste to landfill.
